The Voyager Probes

The Beginning

The Voyager Probes were launched on Aug. 20, 1977 and Sept. 5th 1977. Voyager 1 traveled quicker than it's sister Voyager 2. They both reached Jupiter and Saturn, allowing us to view the outer planets for the first time in greater detail than we have before. Each probe also carries a golden record containing images of life on earth, diagrams of scientific principles, and audio that includes sounds of nature, languages, and music. These are carried in case anyone that might come across the probes can see that there is intelligent life on earth.

45 Years Later

The probes are still in function 45 years later. Though they show their technological age, they only trasmit data 38,000 times slower than a 5G connection, they are still transmitting date from interspellar space. One key discovery was that the heliosphere blocks 70 percent of comsic rays caused by exploding stars.
